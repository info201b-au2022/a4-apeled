This report dissects some of the disparities present in the jail statistics within the United States. Data from the past 50 years is analyzed and presented in the form of visualizations that aim to shine light on inequalities present.

Introduction

This assignment aims at analyzing some of the inequalities present within the incarceration trends in the United States. The dataset analyzed over the course of this assignment contains data about county level prisons. Changes in incarceration and related behavior across the United States over time are enabled for assessment as a result of the data provided in the data set. By analyzing and interpreting that data provided, multiple stakeholders may be able to use and receive impact from the results. Direct stakeholders such as politicians or law enforcers may use this information to actively change the laws that govern our society today, so that less inequality may be present in our future. Indirect stakeholders will hold benefit from the actions of the alterations in guidelines that have led to disparities in the incarceration rates in the United States. This report will provide summary information on the data set analyzed and answer the following prompts with a multitude of visualizations; the Growth of the U.S. Prison Population, the Growth of Prison Population by State, the Growth of Prison Population for Different Genders by Urbanicity, and the Jail Population Ratio (Male to Female) in the Mainland United States.
